Planning Around a Bottleneck: AI-Assisted Event Management

In this group project, our team used AI to solve a real-world wedding planning problem involving 500 invited guests, a 200-seat dining area, a fixed ceremony time of 11:11 a.m., and a hard venue deadline of 1:00 p.m. Rather than asking AI for a generic checklist, we treated the wedding as a system with interconnected constraints and tested whether AI could hold multiple hard limits in view simultaneously to produce an actionable plan.

The key insight — surfaced through structured prompting — was that food quantity was never the bottleneck. Throughput was: how quickly 500 guests could rotate through a 200-seat room in under two hours. The AI helped us design a 4-wave dining schedule, priority seating for elderly and mobility-assisted guests, overflow accommodation strategy, and crisis-response protocols for the 200 unexpected arrivals that brought attendance to 700.

This artifact demonstrates applied AI problem-solving: using precise, constraint- rich prompting across multiple rounds, critically evaluating AI outputs against real-world feasibility, and making human judgment calls where the AI was overconfident. It also shows data management skills — the merged Excel workbook contains 700-row guest-level seating and dining assignments produced across three different AI models.

Skills demonstrated: AI prompt engineering · Constraint-based reasoning · Data organization · Critical evaluation of AI outputs · Collaborative problem-solving
